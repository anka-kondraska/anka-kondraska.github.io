Short Bio

Currently I am a software engineering fellow at Hackbright Academy.

Prior to Hackbright, I immigrated to the U.S. from Poland on my own. I completed my bachelors in NY, and then started work as an office manager. In that role, we had no accounting processes, and I took on accounting projects, teaching myself key accounting practices until I became a self-taught accountant. By the end of that role, I took over management of all finances and created an inventory management system.

After that role, I pivoted into the accounting field and found that I was better able to communicate accounting concepts and use industry tools compared to others who had formal accounting degrees. I continued to grow in the field, working as an accounting consultant for small businesses, training and managing new hires into the field, and serving as the sole accountant at startups that needed a lot of structure and clean up.

In my most recent accounting role, I ran into issues with poor integration between tools that ended in loss of important data. I teamed up with our engineering team to create in-house customizations to solve this issue, and as I worked with them, I realized that I really enjoyed the problem solving and designing custom solutions to solve a real-world problem. That began my interest in software engineering and ultimately lead me here to Hackbright Academy.

Now, I’m looking to apply my strong learning abilities to learn and grow in software engineering as I did in accounting, and work at a company that’s creating solutions to real-world problems.
